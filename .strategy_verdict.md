## Cross-cutting findings (apply to all four)

1. **One regime.** 122 daily bars ≈ 6 months: a Feb–Mar drawdown followed by a strong Apr–Jul rally. Every long-only strategy tested against this tape gets a beta tailwind. Nothing here has seen a bear market, and the two REJECTs demonstrated that raw expectancy on this data is mostly drift (volbreak's baseline: +1.69% per 15-bar hold in validation, all eligible bars, **no signal at all**).
2. **The cost assumption is optimistic.** 0.05%/side ignores NSE delivery STT (0.1%/side), stamp duty, exchange fees, and impact. Realistic all-in for delivery trades is ~0.12–0.20%/side depending on liquidity. That extra ~0.15–0.30% round trip **erases meanrev's +0.39%** and haircuts pullback's +1.05% to roughly +0.75–0.85%. Any promotion decision must re-run with ≥0.15%/side.
3. **Correlated fills ≠ independent samples.** Meanrev's "773 fills, 4 SEs above zero" collapses to 39 signal dates with up to 107 fills in one day. Per-trade SEs on dip-buying strategies are fiction; day-clustered stats are the honest ones.
4. **Portfolio ≠ backtest.** All four take every signal; the live scanner is one-position-per-symbol (all four respect this) but also capital-limited/top-N. On a 107-signal day the live system takes a handful — the realized portfolio is a *selection* from these fills and that selection rule was never tested.

## 1. Pullback continuation — MAYBE (paper-trade)

**Report:** null — the researcher delivered nothing. This is a process failure regardless of strategy quality (flagged separately below). The script `research_pullback.ts` exists and is complete; I ran it and independently replicated it (392 validation fills, +1.052% — exact match).

**Code audit:** Clean. All indicators causal (EMA seeded at SMA, ATR recursive from history start); entry at next-bar open with a 1.5% gap guard; open-below-stop counted as an immediate LOSS with round-trip costs; stop checked before target intrabar; risk >8% of entry discarded; sweep scores train only; last-bar signals discarded. No look-ahead found. Minor conservative quirks (cooldown consumes the full hold window even on NO_FILL) reduce, not inflate, results.

**Recovered results (chosen params, frozen in script):**

| Window | Signals | Fills | Win rate | Expectancy (net) | Max consec losses |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Train (Apr only) | 50 | 39 | 42.9% | +1.32% | 9 |
| Validation | 534 | 392 | 41.1% | **+1.052%** | 16 |

Monthly validation: May +0.95% (193 fills), Jun +2.42% (116), **Jul −0.63% (83)**.

**Independent benchmark check (the key result):** alpha vs equal-weight universe over matched windows = **+0.750%/trade (per-trade t=2.46); day-clustered +1.373% (48 signal dates, t=2.15); avg hold 6.75 bars.** This is the only strategy of the four whose validation edge survives both the drift benchmark and clustering.

**Why not SHIP:**
- Train contained only 39 fills, all in April — the parameter choice is essentially noise; we got a lucky honest OOS test of a semi-arbitrary config, not a tuned-and-confirmed one.
- July decayed to −0.63% (83 fills) — the newest data is the worst.
- 41% win rate with 2R targets and a 16-loss max streak needs strong stomach and position-sizing discipline; live psychology risk is real.
- ₹1 crore turnover floor admits names where 0.05%/side is too thin; at 0.15%/side the edge is ~+0.85%, still positive but untested.
- Single regime, as above.

**Paper-trade spec (exact, from the frozen script):**
- Universe: NSE daily, close ≥ ₹20, close × 20-bar avg volume ≥ ₹1 crore. *(Recommend raising to ₹5 crore for live.)*
- Trend: EMA20 > EMA50, close > EMA50, EMA50 rising over 10 bars, 40-bar high made within last 10 bars. Warmup 55 bars.
- Setup: within last 4 bars a low touched EMA20; pullback low not below EMA50 − 0.25×ATR14; avg volume of those 4 bars < 1.0× 20-bar avg volume.
- Trigger: close > prior bar high, close > open, close > EMA20.
- Entry: next-bar open; NO_FILL if open gaps > 1.5% above signal close, or risk > 8% of entry.
- Stop: min(pullback low − 0.1×ATR, entry − 0.8×ATR); target: entry + 2×risk; timeout: exit at close 15 bars after signal. Stop before target intrabar.
- Promotion criteria: ≥3 months paper, day-clustered alpha vs universe > 0 at t ≥ 2 with costs at 0.15%/side, AND no month worse than −2% avg.

## 2. Mean reversion — MAYBE (weak); paper-trade only, no capital

**Report quality:** High. Honest about degradation, sample limits, regime, and portfolio caveats. Numbers reproduce exactly (train 167 fills / +1.037%; validation 773 fills / +0.394%).

**Code audit:** Clean. Causal indicators; limit-at-signal-close entry credited at limit (pessimistic vs gap-down fills); stop before snap-back intrabar including fill bar; stop credited at stop price with pessimistic entry pairing; costs included; sweep train-only; plateau-not-peak selection documented. Two minor notes, neither material: (a) snap-back executes at the same close that triggers it (standard MOC approximation, slightly optimistic); (b) late-April trades resolve into early May without an embargo (xsmom handled this better) — mild leakage of early-May bars into the tuning objective, direction ambiguous, small.

**Where the report fails:** no market benchmark. My audit: validation trades averaged 4.1-bar holds; the equal-weight universe returned +0.21% over the same windows, so **alpha = +0.187%/trade, t = 1.19 — not significant.** Worse, fills cluster brutally (39 signal dates, max 107 fills/day — dip days are market-wide), and the day-clustered alpha is **−0.155% (t = −0.67)**: on an equal-weighted-by-day basis the strategy *underperformed* the universe. The researcher's "4 standard errors above zero" tests the wrong null (zero instead of drift) with the wrong independence assumption. Add realistic NSE delivery costs (~0.15%/side) and the raw +0.39% goes to roughly +0.19% before any slippage on 107-signal days.

**Verdict:** The methodology is clean and the raw validation expectancy is genuinely positive — this is not a REJECT on the rubric — but what it captured is beta plus a possibly-zero timing residual. Paper-trade only if it costs nothing to run; the promotion criterion is **positive day-clustered alpha vs the universe at realistic costs**, which it currently fails. Do not fund. The researcher's own instincts (kill switch, small size, wait for a correction) were right; the report's headline was not.

## 3. Cross-sectional momentum — REJECT (as-is)

**Report quality:** The best self-audit of the four — the researcher ran the benchmark comparison themselves and correctly concluded no.

**Code audit:** Clean and notably careful: global trading calendar with NaN-aware series; rebalance-level train/embargo/validation classification (prevents stops from leaking straddling trades into train); 96 boundary positions embargoed from both windows; selection guard (≥40 fills, ≥2 independent rebalances) that killed single-rebalance flukes; benchmark computed per rebalance; family-robustness sweep report-only. Regime EMA seeded recursively with warmup. No look-ahead found. The strategy-net-vs-benchmark-gross comparison slightly disadvantages the strategy — honest direction.

**Why REJECT:** +0.62%/position on 965 fills sounds big but the effective sample is 10 independent rebalances; excess over the equal-weight universe is +0.15%/rebalance (t ≈ 0.4); win rate 49.6%; monotonic monthly decay into a negative July. The ranking added nothing over holding the gated universe. With 122 bars the canonical 60–90-bar momentum lookback was infeasible, so this was never a fair test of the family.

**Salvage:** the regime gate (equal-weight universe index vs its EMA50) demonstrably dodged the Feb–Mar drawdown and is a candidate *portfolio-level* kill switch for any long strategy we run — including the pullback paper-trade. Re-test the ranking itself when ≥1 year of candles is cached.

## 4. Volatility breakout — REJECT

**Report quality:** Exemplary negative result. The two controls — market-drift baseline and pre-registered untuned family default — are exactly what the other reports lacked, and they were decisive.

**Code audit:** Clean. Contraction box ends at bar i−1; breakout volume compared to a 20-bar average that excludes bar i; entry next open with chase and open-below-stop guards; gap-through-stop exits at the worse open; stop before target intrabar; costs included; sweep train-only ranked by lower-confidence-bound (mean − 1.5·SE, min 80 fills). No look-ahead found.

**Why REJECT:** Validation +0.209% ± 0.39 (statistically zero) vs a passive drift baseline of +1.69% — the signal actively selected *against* the tape's winners. Untuned control flipped negative (−0.33%). Chosen combo degenerated into a drift harvester (80% timeouts, 12.4% decided win rate). The sweep choosing the loosest value of nearly every filter was correctly read as a red flag. Nothing to salvage on this data; revisit only with multi-year history.
