# Setup Detector Parameter Sweep — holdBars × rMultiple

**Date:** 2026-07-16
**Command:** `npx tsx backend/scripts/backtest_setups.ts --days 180 --holdBars {3,8,12} --rMultiple {1.5,2,3}` (9 runs), plus the pre-existing baseline in `.backtest_results.txt` (holdBars=5, rMultiple=2 per task notes — note the baseline log header records only "5-bar hold", so its rMultiple is not independently verifiable from the log).
**Universe:** 2058 instruments, 180-day lookback, cost 0.05%/side (0.10% round trip).
**Raw stdout of each run:** `.sweep_tmp/hb{H}_r{R}.txt`

`winRatePct` = wins/(wins+losses), ignores timeouts. `expectancyPct` = avg net return per **filled** trade (timeouts included in expectancy, excluded from win rate).

Signal counts fall as holdBars rises because the backtest enforces a re-entry cooldown of `holdBars` bars per instrument — this is a property of the harness, not of the detectors.

## Cross-cutting patterns

1. **Hold time is the dominant axis, and its sign depends on trade direction.** Long setups (MOMENTUM_CONTINUATION, and to a lesser degree LIQUIDITY_SWEEP/BREAKOUT at hb8) improve as hold lengthens; short setups (BEAR_MOMENTUM, BREAKDOWN) deteriorate monotonically. This is upward market drift over Jan–Jul 2026 leaking through timeout exits, not entry-signal skill.
2. **rMultiple barely matters.** At fixed hold, moving R from 1.5→3 shifts expectancy by hundredths of a percent for most setups — wins get bigger but proportionally rarer. The win-rate/target-size trade-off is roughly efficient, i.e. there is no free lunch in stop/target geometry.
3. **Timeouts dominate BREAKOUT, BREAKDOWN, BEAR_MOMENTUM, and BOLLINGER_SQUEEZE** (50–88% of signals). Their stops/targets are far outside typical post-signal ranges, so the "trade" is mostly just holding for N bars. For these setups the measured expectancy is really the expectancy of *drift after the signal*, which is negative — meaning the signals are, if anything, mild contrarian indicators.
4. **No-fill rates are low everywhere** (2–7%), so entry-fill mechanics are not the problem.

## Per-setup verdict — redeemable by parameters alone?

| setup | best config | best expectancy% | redeemable by parameters? |
|---|---|---|---|
| MOMENTUM_CONTINUATION | hb12 / r3 | +0.025 | **Marginal** — only setup to cross zero (hb12/r2 and hb12/r3), but +2 bps/trade vs 10 bps costs is within noise after a 10-config search. Directionally real trend (monotonic in hold), but not tradeable edge on its own. |
| LIQUIDITY_SWEEP | hb8 / r1.5 | -0.372 | **No** — negative in all 10 configs; entries lose ~2:1 regardless of geometry. |
| BEAR_MOMENTUM | hb3 / r1.5 | -0.322 | **No** — best case still loses 32 bps; longer hold strictly worse (short-side drift bleed). |
| BREAKOUT | hb8 / r3 | -0.297 | **No** — negative everywhere, 42–88% timeouts, and n≈200 makes even the best cell unreliable. |
| BOLLINGER_SQUEEZE_BREAKOUT | hb12 / r3 | -1.238 | **No** — deeply negative in every cell; parameters move it by ±0.15pp around -1.4%. |
| BREAKDOWN | hb3 / r1.5 | -1.233 | **No** — 0–7.5% win rates, zero wins in four configs; the signal is close to an inverse indicator. |

**Bottom line:** parameters alone do not redeem any detector. MOMENTUM_CONTINUATION at hb12 is the only candidate worth further work, and only via *entry-quality* improvements (gates/filters on which signals to take), not via more stop/target tuning — axis 2 above shows geometry is a dead end. The five other detectors need their entry logic reworked or removed; in particular the two short setups are fighting period drift, and BREAKOUT/BREAKDOWN's risk units are so wide the backtest barely tests the setup at all.
